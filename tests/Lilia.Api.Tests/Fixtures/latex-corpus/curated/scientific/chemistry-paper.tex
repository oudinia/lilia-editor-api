\section{Introduction}
Hydrogenation adds \ce{H2} across a carbon--carbon double bond in the presence of a metal catalyst such as \ce{Pd} or \ce{Pt}.

\section{Mechanism}
The overall transformation is:
\[
\ce{CH2=CH2 + H2 ->[\text{Pd/C}] CH3-CH3}
\]

The reaction proceeds via surface adsorption of hydrogen onto the metal followed by sequential H-atom transfer.

\section{Selectivity}
Sterically hindered alkenes react more slowly. Competition with isomerisation can reduce selectivity.

\section{Kinetics}
Under excess \ce{H2}, the reaction is pseudo-first-order in alkene:
\[
\text{rate} = k[\text{alkene}]
\]

\section{Conclusion}
Catalyst choice and support matter as much as the alkene itself.
