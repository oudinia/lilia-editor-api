\section{Hypothesis}
We tested whether a simplified checkout flow (variant B) increases conversion over the existing flow (variant A).

$H_0$: $p_A = p_B$ vs $H_1$: $p_A \neq p_B$.

\section{Data}

\begin{tabular}{lrrr}
  \toprule
  Variant & Visitors & Conversions & Rate \\
  \midrule
  A (control) & 12{,}348 & 1{,}247 & 10.10\% \\
  B (treatment) & 12{,}412 & 1{,}389 & 11.19\% \\
  \bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\section{Test statistic}
Using a two-proportion $z$-test,
\[
  z = \frac{\hat p_B - \hat p_A}{\sqrt{\hat p(1-\hat p)(1/n_A + 1/n_B)}} \approx 2.63.
\]

The two-sided $p$-value is $p \approx 0.0086$.

\section{Conclusion}
We reject $H_0$ at the 5\% level. Variant B is associated with a 1.09 percentage-point lift in conversion. 95\% CI on the absolute lift: $[0.28\%, 1.90\%]$.
