\chapter{An Ordinary Afternoon}

The rain had been falling for hours. It drummed against the window in a pattern that was almost musical, though Marta could not have said why. She sat with her tea gone cold, watching the light shift across the wooden floor.

There was nothing to do. Or rather — there was always something to do, but she had decided, quite deliberately, not to do any of it.

\section*{The telephone}

When the telephone rang, she let it ring. It was old enough to ring the way telephones used to ring: a brassy, insistent clatter that filled the whole apartment. After seven rings it stopped. Then it started again.

On the fourteenth ring she picked up.

``Hello?''

``Marta. It's me.''

She closed her eyes. Of course it was him.

\chapter{Evening}

By six o'clock the rain had stopped and the sky had cleared to that particular shade of blue that only happens in April. Marta put on her coat and went out for the first time in two days.

The streets were wet and reflective. She walked without any particular destination.
